6 Things You Can Do to Help

These tips and trick will give you a greater understanding of nature's beauty and give you the perfect photo-ops for sharing on social media/ saving the environment!

Get Out and Explore Nature

If no one leaves their house to take nature pictures for social media, then there won't be any nature pictures!?! You need to "Be the change you wish to see in the world" - Mahatma Gandhi!
You need to get out there and discover the beauty of nature for yourself and spread the word!

Photography: Trevor Leslie

Discover Hidden Beauty

Take a peak around that last corner, you never know what you'll find! There's always a chance for adventure, discovery, learning and wonder! Plus you might get that perfect snap you've been looking for. This spreads awareness and helps the environment!

Photography: Trevor Leslie

Get a Bird's Eye View

To truly understand and marvel at nature's majesty, you need to get up high! Grab a plane or helicopter and take a real look at what you're standing on. It'll humble you, make for great instagram shots and... save the planet...

Photography: Trevor Leslie

Discover a Winter Wondeland

Get outside your comfort zone! Climate change involves winter too. Appreciate the forest during a serene snow-storm and watch as animals struggle to survive in harsh, extreme conditions brought on by climate change. If possible, take a selfie in your winter gear.

Photography: Trevor Leslie

Try Night Time

Shift your perspective and sleep under the stars. Sharing posts of the night sky in the country-side compared to the night-sky in the city and commenting on the light-pollution is a sure-fire way to raise awareness about climate change (and therefore save the planet) and get that sweet karma on reddit.

Photography: Trevor Leslie

See the Ugly, Along with the Beautiful

Look for the ugly, as well as the breathtaking. Appreciating nature isn't all sunshine and flowers. Really take in the devastation that comes from mankind's unsustainable practices such as deforestation and displacing of natural habitats. This would be a great for Twitter.

Photography: Trevor Leslie
